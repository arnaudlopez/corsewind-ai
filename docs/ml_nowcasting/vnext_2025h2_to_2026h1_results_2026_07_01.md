## Interpretation

The new physical signals are useful, but not sufficient as a direct replacement model. They reduce error versus the older feature set on both targets, with larger gains in high wind/high gust regimes. That means the feature work is scientifically useful, but the current v_next LightGBM training recipe is not yet the best production recipe.

This is consistent with the earlier error-floor diagnosis: the easiest gains from adding features are smaller than the remaining gap to `0.9 RMSE`, because part of the error is label volatility, spot heterogeneity, regime transitions, and horizon-specific behavior.

## Next Decision

Use v_next as an input to the next champion-search layer, not as the champion itself:

1. First generate dense same-key champion predictions for the v_next evaluation rows.
2. Test conservative blends only, not an unconstrained flexible router.
3. Add regime routing for strong wind/gust thresholds: `12`, `15`, `20`, `25 kt`.
4. Train horizon-specific or horizon-weighted specialists, especially `15` and `30 min`.
5. Evaluate only on strict common rows and keep separate scorecards for global, strong wind, strong gust, thermal regime, spot, and lead.
6. Promote only if the blend beats:
   - wind champion: `RMSE < 1.268019`
   - gust champion: `RMSE < 1.484221`

Guardrail from earlier router work:

- `docs/ml_nowcasting/router_oof_base_lgbm_results_2026_07_01.md` showed that flexible routers can over-switch and degrade when candidate overlap is sparse.
- The previous conservative blend around the champion was stable but only noise-level better on gust and worse on wind.
- Therefore v_next should be tested as a constrained expert delta around the champion, not as a free router replacement.
